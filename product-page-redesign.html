Product Page Case Study

Redesigning a product page to increase purchase confidence.

Overview

The product page had important information, but it was not ordered around buyer decision-making. Benefits, usage, trust, reviews, and objections were scattered, which made the purchase feel less obvious.

+18% Placeholder Add to Cart improvement
+11% CTA engagement
+9% Subscription selection

Goals

  • Improve the buy box hierarchy and CTA visibility.
  • Explain the product benefits before asking users to scroll deeply.
  • Answer objections around trust, delivery, ingredients, subscription, or guarantee.
  • Make mobile purchasing feel simpler and faster.

Research and audit findings

Before designing, I looked for points where users could misunderstand the value, lose confidence, or miss the next action.

  • Reviewed the product page using a buyer objection checklist.
  • Mapped what a first-time visitor needs to know before clicking Add to Cart.
  • Checked mobile layout and sticky CTA opportunities.
  • Audited product copy for clarity, specificity, and outcome-driven messaging.

Design decisions

The final design direction was focused on reducing decision friction and making the next step obvious.

  • Reordered the page into benefit, proof, mechanism, comparison, FAQ, and CTA sections.
  • Improved the buy box with clearer price, offer, delivery note, and guarantee text.
  • Added trust badges and review snippets near the first CTA.
  • Created scannable FAQ and objection-handling sections.
  • Designed a mobile-friendly sticky CTA pattern for lower friction.

Result and lesson

A product page sells better when it answers the buyer's questions in the order they naturally appear.

What I would test next

  • Hero headline clarity against the original version.
  • CTA copy and placement across desktop and mobile.
  • Social proof position and format.
  • Section order based on user scroll behavior.
  • Mobile friction around the final conversion step.
