Overview

The homepage looked visually busy, but it did not communicate the offer fast enough. New visitors had to scroll too much before understanding what the brand sold, why it mattered, and what action to take next.

+21% Placeholder add-to-cart lift
+14% Product page click-through
-28% Homepage confusion signals

Goals

  • Make the offer and product category clear within the first few seconds.
  • Move trust signals and social proof closer to the top of the page.
  • Improve CTA clarity across desktop and mobile.
  • Create a stronger path from homepage to product discovery.

Research and audit findings

Before designing, I looked for points where users could misunderstand the value, lose confidence, or miss the next action.

  • Reviewed the above-the-fold section for message clarity, contrast, hierarchy, and CTA strength.
  • Mapped the visitor journey from first impression to product page click.
  • Identified low-confidence sections where users needed proof, benefits, or clearer product education.
  • Checked mobile layout density because most ecommerce visitors make quick decisions on small screens.

Design decisions

The final design direction was focused on reducing decision friction and making the next step obvious.

  • Rebuilt the hero around a benefit-led headline and immediate offer clarity.
  • Added social proof above the fold to reduce first-time buyer hesitation.
  • Grouped benefits into scannable cards instead of long copy blocks.
  • Used product imagery to support the message rather than decorate the page.
  • Placed a low-friction secondary action for users not ready to buy immediately.

Result and lesson

A homepage should not only look premium. It should quickly explain the offer, reduce uncertainty, and guide the visitor to the next buying step.

  • Hero headline clarity against the original version.
  • CTA copy and placement across desktop and mobile.
  • Social proof position and format.
  • Section order based on user scroll behavior.
  • Mobile friction around the final conversion step.
